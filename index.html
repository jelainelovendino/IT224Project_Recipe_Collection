Tokwa't Baboy

Tofu and pork in vinegar.

Tokwa't Baboy

Ingredients

  • 1 cup small shrimps
  • 1 cup cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 1 1/2 cups mung bean
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 piece raw egg
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 1/4 cups water
  • 2 cups cooking oil

Intructions:

  1. Combine cornstarch, flour, baking powder, egg, water, salt, and ground black pepper then stir until diluted.
  2. Add the shrimps and mung bean sprouts in the mixture and lightly stir to distribute.
  3. Heat a frying pan or small cooking pot and pour-in the cooking oil.
  4. When the oil is hot enough, scoop about 2-3 tablespoons of the mixture and quickly drop it in hot oil.
  5. Cook each side fir 2-3 minutes or until the color turn golden brown. Please note that cooking time may vary. Repeat this step until the entire mixture is consumed.
  6. Remove the deep-fried ukoy from the pan and place in a palte with paper towel to absorb the oil.
  7. Remove the paper towel and serve.
  8. Share and enjoy!

Lumpia

Filling Ingredient

  • 2 pieces carrot

Chicharon Bulaklak

Savory and Crispy Chicharon.

Chicharon Bulaklak

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. ruffle fat
  • 1 tablespoons whole peppercorn.
  • 5 dried bay leaves.
  • 2 1/2 tablespoons sea salt.
  • 4 cups canola oil.
  • 6 cups water.

Intructions:

  1. Arrange the ruffle fat, whole peppercorn, 2 tablespoons salt, and bay leaves in a cooking pot. Pour-in water. Let boil. Cover and then simmer for 1 hour.
  2. Turn the heat off. Remove the boiled ruffle fat from the pot and then let the water drip-off.
  3. Rub the remaining salt all over the ruffled fat. Set aside.
  4. Meanwhile, heat the cooking oil in a deep fryer or a deep cooking pot.
  5. When the oil gets hot, slowly add-in the ruffled fat. Deep fry in low to medium heat until the texture becomes crisp and the color turns medium brown.
  6. Remove from the deep fryer or cooking pot. Arrange in a plate lined with paper towels to absorb the oil.
  7. Serve with spiced vinegar.
  8. Share and enjoy!

Kinilaw na Tanigue

Citrus-cured fish with spice.

Kinilaw na Tanigue

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. Tanigue
  • 2 thumbs fresh ginger
  • 8 pieces hot chili
  • 15 to 20 pieces calamansi
  • 1 medium red onion
  • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon sugar
  • Salt to tast

Intructions:

  1. Squeeze the juice out of the calamansi over a large bowl. Use a sieve to filter the seeds. Discard the seeds.
  2. In the bowl with calamansi juice, add sugar, ½ teaspoon salt, and ground black pepper. Stir until the sugar and salt are diluted
  3. Add the ginger, chili, and onion. Stir for a few seconds until all ingredients are well blended
  4. Arranged the raw fish cubes in a large bowl. Pour the calamansi mixture. Toss until the fish cubes are coated. Let is stay for 10 minutes.
  5. Toss one more time and taste. Add more salt if needed. You can also add more calamansi juice if needed. Toss to blend all the ingredients. Securely cover the bowl and place it inside the refrigerator. Let it chill for at least 3 hours
  6. Serve. Share and enjoy!

Arroz Caldo

Savory rice porridge with chicken

Arroz Caldo

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken
  • 1 1/2 cups rice uncooked
  • 1 Maggi Magic Sarap Chivken Cube
  • 34 ounces water
  • 8 cloves garlic
  • 3 onions
  • 5 eggs
  • 1 cup green onions
  • 3 thumbs ginger

  • 1 lemon or 4 pieces of calamansi
  • Fish sauce and ground black pepper to taste
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil

Intructions:

  1. In a pot, heat the cooking oil, then sauté the garlic, onion, and ginger.
  2. Add the chicken. Sauté until the outer layer turns golden brown.
  3. Add the Add the uncooked rice. Sauté for 1 minute.
  4. Pour in the water and bring to a boil.
  5. Add the Maggi Magic Chicken Cube. Stir. Simmer until the rice is fully cooked (about 30 to 40 minutes). Note: Make sure to occasionally stir the mixture to prevent the ingredients from sticking to the bottom of the pan.
  6. Add the safflower and season with fish sauce. Stir.
  7. Add the hard-boiled eggs.
  8. ransfer to a bowl. Top with garlic, green onions, and squeeze some calamansi. Share and enjoy!
Bibingka

Soft rice cake with coconut topping

Bibingka

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt
  • 2 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1/2 granulated sugar
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup fresh milk
  • 1 piece salted duck egg
  • 1/2 cup grated cheese
  • 3 pieces raw eggs
  • 1/4 cup grayed coconut
  • pre-cut banana leaf

Intructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Combine rice flour, baking powder, and salt then mix well. Set aside.
  3. Cream butter then gradually put-in sugar while whisking.
  4. Add the eggs then whisk until every ingredient is well incorporated
  5. Gradually add the rice flour, salt, and baking powder mixture then continue mixing.
  6. Pour-in coconut milk and fresh milk then whisk some more for 1 to 2 minutes.
  7. Pour the mixture on the pan
  8. Bake for 15 minutes.
  9. Remove from the oven then top with sliced salted egg and grated cheese (do not turn the oven off).
  10. Put back in the oven and bake for 15 to 20 minutes or until the color of the top turn medium brown.
  11. Remove from the oven and let cool.
  12. Brush with butter and top with grated coconut.
  13. Serve. Share and enjoy!
Ube Champorado

Sweet purple rice porridge

Ube Champorado

Ingredients

  • 1 250-gram cup malagkit rice rinsed
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 Cup ube halaya
  • 1 teaspoon ube flavoring
  • 1/2 Cup coconut cream plus more to serve
  • evaporated milk to taste
  • condensed milk to taste
  • Salt to taste

Intructions:

  1. Pour rice and water in a medium stockpot over medium heat. Bring to a boil then lower to a simmer until rice is cooked, stirring regularly to avoid scorching and sticking. Add more water if necessary to achieve your desired consistency.
  2. Stir in ube halaya, flavoring, coconut cream, and a pinch of salt, to taste, once rice is tender. Cook, stirring, until heated through and ube is mixed in well.
  3. Ladle champorado into bowls. Top with a swirl each of evaporated milk or condensed milk to taste.
Longsilog

Sweet sausage, garlic rice, fried egg

Longsilog

Ingredients

  • 1/2 lb. skinless longanisa
  • F2 pieces egg
  • 4 cups rice leftover
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 4 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt and ground black pepper to taste

Intructions:

  1. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan. Once the oil gets hot, fry the eggs. Remove from the pan. Set aside.
  2. Add remaining oil in the pan. Fry the longanisa until the outer part turns light brown (around 1 1/2 minutes).
  3. Pour-in water. Let boil. Continue boiling until the water evaporates. Fry the longanisa in remaining oil until fully cooked. Remove from the pan and set aside
  4. Using the remaining oil, cook garlic until it starts to turn light brown.

  6. Assemble the fried eggs, longanisa, and sinangag on a plate. Serve with spicy vinegar as a dipping sauce for the longanisa.

Tapsilog

Marinated beef, garlic rice, fried egg

Tapsilog

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. beef sirloin, sliced thinly
  • 3 pieces eggs
  • 6 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 3 tablespoons Knorr Liquid Seasoning
  • 6 cloves crushed garlic
  • ¾ cups pineapple juice
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon ground white pepper
  • 5 cups leftover rice
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 5 cloves garlic, crushed

Intructions:

  1. Prepare the tapa by placing the beef in a large bowl. Combine with all the tapa marinade ingredients. Mix well and cover the bowl. Place inside the fridge and marinate overnight.
  2. Cook the garlic fried rice (sinangag na kanin) by heating 3 tablespoons cooking oil in a pan. Add crushed garlic. Cook until garlic turns light brown. Add the leftover rice. Stir-fry for 3 minutes.
  3. Season with salt. Continue to stir-fry for 3 to 5 minutes. Set aside.
  4. Start to cook the tapa. Heat a pan and pour the marinated beef into it, including the marinade. Add ¾ cups water. Let the mixture boil. Cover the pan and continue to cook until the liquid reduces to half. Add 3 tablespoons cooking oil into the mixture. Continue to cook until the liquid completely evaporates. Fry the beef tapa in remaining oil until medium brown. Set aside.
  5. Fry the egg by pouring 1 tablespoon oil on a pan. Crack a piece of egg and sprinkle enough salt on top. Cook for 30 seconds. Pour 2 tablespoons water on the side of the pan. Cover and let the water boil. Continue to cook until the egg yolk gets completely cooked by the steam.
  6. Arrange the beef tapa, sinangag, and fried egg on a large plate to form Tapsilog. Serve with vinegar as dipping sauce for tapa.
Sopas

Creamy macaroni soup with chicken

Sopas

Ingredients

  • 12 oz. chicken breast
  • ½ piece cabbage
  • 3 stalks celery
  • 1 piece onion
  • 3 pieces hotdog
  • ¾ cups carrots
  • 12 oz. evaporated milk
  • 1 ½ cups elbow macaroni
  • 2 teaspoons minced garlic
  • 1 piece Knorr Chicken Cube
  • 6 to 8 cups water
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt and ground black pepper to taste

Intructions:

  1. Boil 3 cups water in a medium pot. Add the chicken breasts.
  2. Cover and cook in medium heat for 15 to 18 minutes. You may add more water if needed.
  3. Separate the chicken from the water. Let the chicken cool down and then shred. Set both shredded chicken and water aside
  4. Heat oil in a large soup pot. Sauté the garlic until it turns medium brown. Add onion, celery, and carrot. Continue to sauté for 2 to 3 minutes.
  5. Add the shredded chicken. Stir and cook for 1 minute.
  6. Add the hot dog. Quickly stir and then pour the water used to boil the chicken. Also pour the remaining water. Let boil.
  7. Add the Knorr Chicken Cube. Stir. Cover and cook for 3 to 5 minutes.
  8. Put the elbow macaroni in the pot. Cook for 10 minutes.
  9. Add the cabbage. Stir and cook for 3 to 5 minutes. Note: you can add more water if needed.
  10. Pour the evaporated milk. Stir, let boil, cover and cook for another 3 minutes.
  11. Add salt and ground black pepper to taste.
  12. Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve.
  13. Share and enjoy!

Beef Mechado

Slow-cooked beef in tomato sauce

Beef Mechado

Ingredients

  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 piece large onion, sliced
  • 2 lbs beef chuck, cubed
  • 8 ounces tomato sauce
  • 1 cup water
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 slice lemon with rind
  • 1 piece large potato, sliced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 pieces bay leaves (laurel)
  • Salt to taste

Intructions:

  1. Heat cooking oil in a pan then sauté the garlic and onion.
  2. Put in the beef and sauté for about 3 minutes or until the color turns light brown.
  3. Add the tomato sauce and water then simmer until the meat is tender. Add water as needed. Note: this can take 60 to 120 minutes depending on the quality of the beef.
  4. Add the soy sauce, ground black pepper, lemon rind, laurel leaves, and salt then simmer until excess liquid evaporates.
  5. Put in the potatoes and cook until the potatoes are soft.
  6. Place in a serving plate then serve hot with rice. Share and Enjoy!
Bicol Express

Spicy pork cooked in coconut milk

Bicol Express

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. pork belly, sliced into strips
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 2 cups coconut cream
  • 1/4 cup shrimp paste or salted krill
  • 5 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 5 pieces Thai chili pepper, chopped
  • 2 thumbs ginger, minced
  • 1 piece onion, chopped
  • 2 pieces Serrano pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup water (optional)

Intructions:

  4. Transfer to a serving plate and serve with warm rice.
Crispy Pata

Crispy fried pork with tender meat

Crispy Pata

Ingredients

  • 1 piece whole pig leg, cleaned
  • 6 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 2 tablespoons whole peppercorn
  • 4 pieces star anise (optional)
  • 6 teaspoons salt
  • 2 teaspoons ground black pepper
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 12 cups water
  • 6 cups cooking oil

Intructions:

  1. Pour water in a cooking pot then let it boil.
  2. Put in dried bay leaves, whole peppercorn, star anise, and 4 teaspoons of salt.
  3. Add the whole pig’s leg to the cooking pot then simmer until the leg becomes tender (about 45 to 60 minutes).
  4. Remove the tender leg from the cooking pot and set aside until the temperature goes down.
  5. Rub the leg with garlic powder, ground black pepper, and remaining salt. Let stand for 15 minutes to absorb the rub.
  6. Heat a clean large cooking pot (preferably with a cover) and pour in cooking oil.
  7. When the oil becomes hot, deep fry the rubbed pork leg. Continue cooking on medium heat until one side becomes crispy, then cautiously flip the leg to crisp the other side. Note: Be extra careful in doing this procedure.
  8. Turn off the heat; remove the crispy pork leg; and transfer it to a wide serving plate.
  9. Serve with atcharang papaya and soy sauce–vinegar dipping sauce.
  10. Share and enjoy!
Pakbet

Stewed vegetable with shrimp paste flavor.

Pakbet

Ingredients

  • 1 lb lechon kawali, sliced
  • 1 piece Knorr Shrimp Cube
  • 12 pieces sitaw, cut into 2-inch length
  • 1/2 piece kalabasa, cubed
  • 12 pieces okra
  • 1 piece Chinese eggplant, sliced
  • 1 piece ampalaya, sliced
  • 1 piece kamote, cubed (optional)
  • 2 pieces tomato, cubed
  • 2 thumbs ginger, crushed (optional)
  • 1 piece onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2 teaspoons bagoong alamang
  • 2 ½ cups water
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper

Intructions:

  1. Heat cooking oil in a pot. Sauté onion and garlic. Add ginger and continue to cook until the onion softens.
  2. Add lechon kawali. Sauté for 1 minute.
  3. Pour water. Let it boil.
  4. Add Knorr Shrimp Cube. Cover the pot and cook on medium heat for 20 minutes.
  5. Add tomato and bagoong alamang. Stir. Cover the pot and cook for 3 minutes.
  6. Put the kalabasa and kamote into the pot. Cook for 7 minutes.
  7. Add sitaw, okra, ampalaya, and eggplant. Stir. Cover the pot and cook for 5 minutes.
  8. Season with ground black pepper and add the remaining lechon kawali. Cook for a few more minutes.
  9. Transfer to a serving plate. Serve. Share and enjoy!
Pork Menudo sa Gata

Pork Stew with liver and coconut

Pork menudo sa Gata

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ lbs. pork shoulder, cut into cubes
  • 1 piece Knorr Pork Cube
  • 4 ounces pork liver, cubed
  • 2 pieces potato, cubed
  • 1 piece carrot, cubed
  • 4 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 8 ounces tomato sauce
  • 1 piece onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • Salt and ground black pepper to taste

Intructions:

  1. Heat oil in a wok. Sauté garlic and onion until the onion softens.
  2. Add pork. Continue sautéing until the outer part turns light brown.
  3. Add soy sauce. Cook for 1 minute.
  4. Pour tomato sauce and coconut milk. Let it boil.
  5. Add Knorr Pork Cube. Stir and then add dried bay leaves. Cover the wok. Adjust heat to a low setting. Continue cooking until the pork tenderizes. Note: Add water if the sauce starts to dry up while the pork is not tender yet.
  6. Add pork liver. Cook for 8 minutes.
  7. Add potato and carrot. Cook for 10 minutes.
  8. Season with salt and ground black pepper. Serve with warm rice.
  9. Share and enjoy!
Relyenong Bangus

Stuffed milkfish with savory filling

Relyenong Bangus

Ingredients

  • 2 pcs medium-sized bangus (milkfish)
  • 1 pc onion, chopped finely
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pc large-sized carrot, cubed
  • 1 cup raisins
  • 2 pcs raw eggs, large
  • 1/8 cup oyster sauce
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 pcs red bell pepper, chopped finely
  • 1/8 cup flour
  • 1 cup frozen green peas
  • 4 pcs kalamansi, juice extracted
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 kilo ground pork
  • 1 cup water (for boiling the meat)
  • Cooking oil for frying
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Intructions:

  1. Scrape fish scales and clean the fish. Gently pound to loosen meat from the skin using the flat side of a knife.
  2. Break the big bone at the nape and tail. Insert the handle of an aluminum kitchen turner (sandok) through the fish neck.
  3. Gently scrape down the handle between the meat and the skin, working towards the tail and around the fish.
  4. Once the meat is separated, remove the handle and squeeze the meat out from the tail through the head without cutting the skin.
  5. Marinate the fish skin and head with soy sauce and calamansi juice. Set aside.
  6. Boil the fish meat in a cup of water, then drain. Remove bones and flake the meat.
  7. Heat 1 tbsp cooking oil in a pan and sauté ground pork. Add 1 cup of water and simmer until the liquid evaporates. Continue sautéing until browned, then set aside.
  8. Sauté garlic until brown. Add onion and carrot, stirring for 2 minutes.
  9. Stir in fish meat, green peas, raisins, ground pork, and bell pepper.
  10. Season with salt, ground pepper, oyster sauce, and Worcestershire sauce. Sauté for a few minutes, then turn off the heat.
  11. Transfer the cooked mixture to a plate and let it cool. Mix in raw egg and flour thoroughly.
  12. Stuff the mixture into the bangus skin, then sew the head opening with a needle and thread.
  13. Dredge the bangus in flour until well coated, then fry until golden brown.
  14. Cool before slicing. Garnish with sliced fresh tomato, spring onions, or parsley.
  15. Serve with catsup.

Pork Adobo

Slow-cooked soy sauce and vinegar stew

Pork Adobo

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly
  • 2 tablespoons garlic
  • 5 dried bay leaves
  • 4 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon peppercorn
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste

Intructions:

  1. Combine the pork belly, soy sauce, and garlic then marinate for at least 1 hour.
  2. Heat the pot and put in the marinated pork belly, then cook for a few minutes.
  3. Pour remaining marinade including garlic.
  4. Add water, whole peppercorn, and dried bay leaves then bring to a boil. Simmer for 40 minutes to 1 hour.
  5. Put in the vinegar and simmer for 12 to 15 minutes.
  6. Add salt to taste.
  7. Serve hot. Share and enjoy!
Pata Hamonada

Braised pork leg in sweet sauce

Pata Hamonada

Ingredients

  • 3 ½ lbs pork hock (pata), sliced or whole
  • 1 piece Knorr Pork Cube
  • 20 ounces pineapple chunks (canned)
  • 1 piece onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, crushed
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 5 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • 1 teaspoon whole peppercorn
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt to taste

Intructions:

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a cooking pot. Add pork hock. Let boil for 15 minutes. Discard water and set the pork aside.
  2. Heat cooking oil in a cooking pot. Sauté garlic and onion.
  3. Add the boiled pork hock once the onion softens.
  4. Pour soy sauce, pineapple juice, and water. Let it boil.
  5. Add whole peppercorn and dried bay leaves.
  6. Pressure cook for 18 minutes. Note: If not using a pressure cooker, boil the pork for 90 minutes in a regular cooking pot. Make sure to add water if the liquid is about to dry up.
  7. Add Knorr Pork Cube, brown sugar, and pineapple chunks. Stir and let it simmer until the sauce reduces to half.
  8. Season with salt.
  9. Transfer to a serving plate. Serve with rice. Share and enjoy!
Kare Kare

Peanut sauce stew with oxtail

Kare kare

Ingredients

  • lbs oxtail, cut into 2-inch slices (you can also use tripe or beef slices)
  • 1 piece small banana flower bud, sliced
  • 1 bundle pechay or bok choy
  • 1 bundle string beans, cut into 2-inch slices
  • 4 pieces eggplants, sliced
  • 1 cup ground peanuts
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1/2 cup shrimp paste
  • 34 ounces water (about 1 liter)
  • 1/2 cup annatto seeds, soaked in a cup of water
  • 1/2 cup toasted ground rice
  • 1 tablespoon garlic, minced
  • 1 piece onion, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Intructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring the water to a boil.
  2. Put in the oxtail followed by the onions and simmer for 2.5 to 3 hours or until tender (35 minutes if using a pressure cooker).
  3. Once the meat is tender, add the ground peanuts, peanut butter, and coloring (water from the annatto seed mixture) and simmer for 5 to 7 minutes.
  4. Add the toasted ground rice and simmer for 5 minutes.
  5. In a separate pan, sauté the garlic, then add the banana flower, eggplant, and string beans. Cook for 5 minutes.
  6. Transfer the cooked vegetables to the large pot (where the rest of the ingredients are).
  7. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot with shrimp paste. Enjoy!
Beef Bulalo

Hearty beef soup with bone marrow

Beef Bulalo

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ lbs beef shank (bulalo)
  • 1 Knorr beef cube
  • 6 to 8 leaves Napa cabbage
  • 1 sweet corn, cut into 5 small pieces
  • 15 to 18 pieces green beans
  • 1 medium yellow onion, wedged
  • 1 tablespoon whole peppercorn
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce (patis)
  • 8 cups water

Intructions:

  1. Boil water in a pressure cooker.
  2. Add onions and whole peppercorn. Cook for 3 minutes.
  3. Add beef shank and let it boil.
  4. Add Knorr beef cube. Stir, then cover the pressure cooker and cook for 40 to 45 minutes.
  5. Remove the cover once the pressure is released. Add corn and cook for 15 minutes.
  6. Put the green beans into the cooker and pour in the fish sauce. Stir and continue to cook for 5 minutes.
  7. Arrange fresh Napa cabbage (pechay baguio) in a serving bowl.
  8. Transfer the contents of the pressure cooker into the same bowl.
  9. Serve hot with patis, calamansi, and siling labuyo.
  10. Share and enjoy!
Sinigang

Saur and savory meat stew

Sinigang

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly
  • 1 lb young tamarind
  • 1 bunch water spinach, chopped
  • 8 pieces string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 pieces eggplants, sliced
  • 1 piece daikon radish
  • 8 pieces okras
  • 2 pieces tomatoes, sliced into wedges
  • 2 pieces long green pepper
  • 1 piece onion, sliced into wedges
  • 2 quarts water
  • Fish sauce and ground black pepper to taste

Intructions:

  1. Boil the young tamarind in 2 quarts of water for 40 minutes.
  2. Filter the tamarind broth using a kitchen sieve or strainer. Squeeze the tamarind to extract its remaining juices.
  3. Pour the tamarind broth into a cooking pot. Let it boil, then add the onion, pork belly, and half of the tomatoes.
  4. Skim off the floating scums, pour 1 tablespoon fish sauce, cover, and continue to simmer for 1 hour.
  5. Add daikon radish and eggplants. Cook for 5 minutes.
  6. Add long green pepper, string beans, remaining tomatoes, and okra. Cook for 3 minutes.
  7. Add the chopped water spinach stalks and season with fish sauce and ground black pepper. Cook for 2 minutes.
  8. Put in the water spinach leaves. Cover and turn off the heat. Let the residual heat cook the leaves for 3 minutes before serving.
  9. Share and enjoy!
Pocherong Bisaya

Sweet and savory pork broth

Pocherong Bisaya

Ingredients

  • 1 kilo beef (use shank with bone marrow)
  • Oil for sautéing
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 1 cup leeks, sliced
  • 10 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 4 stalks lemongrass (tanglad), smashed
  • 1 (1-inch) piece ginger, peeled
  • 2 ears fresh sweet corn, sliced into 2 cobs
  • 1 cup fresh bamboo shoots
  • 1 beef bouillon cube
  • 1 potato, peeled and sliced in half
  • 2 bunches pechay leaves
  • 1 cabbage, quartered
  • Salt to taste
  • Soy sauce to serve

Intructions:

  1. Boil the beef shank in a pot of water for 5 minutes. Skim off any impurities that rise to the surface. Strain and set beef aside.
  2. Heat oil and sauté onions, leeks, garlic, lemongrass, and ginger in a pot over medium heat.
  3. Add the beef shanks and enough water to cover. Add corn and bamboo shoots, if using.
  4. Simmer for 2 to 3 hours or until the beef is tender.
  5. When the beef is tender, strain the broth into a bowl. Discard any grit accumulated in the strainer.
  6. Return the clear broth to the pot and add beef bouillon cube (if using), potato, pechay, and cabbage.
  7. Simmer until potatoes are tender. Season with salt and pepper.
  8. Serve hot with a dipping sauce of soy sauce, siling labuyo, and calamansi.
